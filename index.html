Maize was introduced to Africa from the Americas between the 16th and 17th century. Before this, sorghum and millet were the staple cereals in most of Sub-Saharan Africa. Maize was readily accepted by African farmers as its cultivation was very similar to that of sorghum but with significantly higher yields. Eventually, maize displaced sorghum as the primary cereal in all but the drier regions. The full replacement of these crops with maize took place in the latter half of the twentieth century.
Pilau is a glorified combination of rice cooked with flavor bursting spices like cumin, cardamon, cinnamon, and cloves. The fragrant rice is fantastic to eat with a form of meat stew and a few slices of fresh tomato and onions. Biriyani is another form of spiced rice that is a popular Kenyan food on the coast. Even though it’s in Tanzania, I’ve enjoyed some of the best pilau and biryani I’ve ever eaten on the island of Zanzibar!

Kenya, an African country with major higher plant diversity, has a corresponding diversity of plant associations, because of the wide geographic distribution, diverse climatic conditions and soil types. In this article, all families and genera were revised using recent molecular systematics research, forming a "Synoptic List of Families and Genera of Kenyan Vascular Plants (SLFGKVP)". In total, there are 225 families, 1538 genera and 6293 indigenous species and and 62 families, 302 genera and 588 exotic species in Kenya.
Kenya’s diverse ecosystems are in danger. Drought, climate change, deforestation, population pressures and inadequate government oversight all challenge sustainability. Key flagship species such as the African elephant, rhinoceros and lion are at critical crossroads for survival. Other endangered mammals include roan, sable, sitatunga, Grevy’s zebra and wild dog. Yet significant strides in conservation continue, particularly through public-private partnerships.

Kenya’s tremendous biodiversity includes 25,000 different animal species. In the Maasai Mara, the plains darken during the annual wildebeest migration, with predators in stealthy pursuit. The reserve has plenty of cheetah but is above all the Lion Kingdom, with large prides holding court. Other carnivores include spotted hyena, jackal and bat-eared fox. WHAT TO SEE IN KENYA

NAIROBI

Two unavoidable visits in the Kenyan capital: the National Museum that exhibits an impressive collection of rock art; and the house museum of Karen Blixen, the Danish writer who lived on a farm on the outskirts of the city and recounted her experiences in Kenya in her stories. How can one not evoke African landscapes again and again after having seen the film Out of Africa (1985), based on his life and his work? The residence in which Blixen lived has many original pieces and some of the furniture.

NAIROBI

MOUNT KENIA NATIONAL PARK

The second highest peak in Africa (5,199 meters), after Kilimanjaro, presides over this very interesting reserve due to the variety of vegetation and the contrast of the landscape of the Highlands with those of the rest of the country. The cost depends a lot on the duration of the climb. For a guided climb to Lenana Peak, you're looking at a cost of around $2,000-$4,000 USD. This cost generally includes food and accommodation, both in Nairobi and on Mount Kenya, as well as necessary technical equipment.

AMBOSELI NATIONAL PARK

Kilimanjaro, of volcanic origin and the highest mountain in Africa (5,895 m), is the symbol of the most famous park in Kenya. Spectacular for the herds of elephants, you can fly over in a balloon to appreciate its magnitude and beauty. In the image, a Masai village, made up of manyhattas - mud huts and branches - protected by a palisade of thorns. In Kenya live about a million and a half Maasai. The western area is dominated by the immense Amboseli Lake, which dries up outside of the rainy season.

MASAI MARA NATIONAL PARK

"If you try to imagine Kenya without ever having traveled there, what you are imagining is the Masai Mara," says the Kenya Tourism page, and this park is the paradigm of the African reserve. Located west of the Rift Valley and the natural continuation of the Serengeti plains in Tanzania, its vast plains are the territory of big cats and huge herds of herbivores. The landscape is made up of grassy plains and rolling hills, running Talek rivers. The nearby area is dotted with villages of the Maasai people.

LAKE NAKURU NATIONAL PARK

It is an immense and valuable breeding and resting place for flamingos and other aquatic birds and one of the ornithological paradises on the planet. The millions of pink flamingos on its banks stand out. Lake Nakuru is only worth visiting if you have not yet gone to the Masai Mara or one of the other larger parks. It will feel anticlimactic if you visit it after spending several days in one of the other larger national parks like the Masai Mara and the long drive to get there may not be with it for you.

LAKE TURKANA

Made up of three national parks, it is a major destination for migratory waterfowl and a breeding ground for the Nile crocodile and hippopotamus. Its exceptional characteristics, it is the largest saline lake in Africa, earned it to be declared a World Heritage Site in 1997.

SAMBURU RESERVE

The Samburu National Reserve is a game reserve on the banks of the Ewaso Ng'iro river in Kenya. On the other side of the river is the Buffalo Springs National Reserve. The park is 165 km² in size and is situated 350 kilometers from Nairobi. It ranges in altitude from 800 to 1230m above sea level. Geographically, it is located in Samburu County. In northeastern Kenya, this small protected area is home to reticulated giraffes and grevy zebras. The film La masai blanca (2005) was filmed in this place.

TSAVO WESTERN NATIONAL PARK

Tsavo West National Park is located in the Coast Province of Kenya. The park covers an area of 9,065 square kilometres. The A109 road Nairobi-Mombasa and a railway divides it from the adjoining Tsavo East National Park. Together with adjoining ranches and protected areas, they comprise the Tsavo Conservation Area. Crocodiles and hippos can be seen in the Mzima Springs Pond. The excursion to the Roaring Rocks is highly recommended due to the impressive views it offers.

LAMU

Located off the northeastern coast of Kenya, this archipelago exudes beauty with its beaches bathed by the Indian Ocean and its white-washed towns where Portuguese and Muslim influences can be seen. Lamu is the main island, where the most prominent city is located, founded in the 14th century, which also bears the name of Lamu. In the image, the town of Shela, on the island of Lamu. Lamu's urban structure clearly demonstrates the influences that coalesced over the centuries.
